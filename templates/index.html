Explainability Framework: How Grad-CAM Addresses the Clinical Trust Bottleneck

Clinical trust in AI is hampered by the "black-box" problem: a model predicts an anomaly but cannot explain why. If doctors cannot inspect the reasoning, they cannot safely rely on it for critical triage decisions.

Grad-CAM (Gradient-weighted Class Activation Mapping) uses the gradients flowing into the final convolutional layer of ResNet-50 (`layer4`) to produce a coarse localization map. By taking the gradients of the target class score with respect to the feature map activations, we obtain importance weights for each filter:

$$\alpha_c^k = \frac{1}{Z} \sum_{i} \sum_{j} \frac{\partial Y^c}{\partial A_{i,j}^k}$$ $$L_{\text{Grad-CAM}}^c = \text{ReLU}\left(\sum_{k} \alpha_c^k A^k\right)$$

This identifies the feature maps that contribute positively to the output class. The resulting activation map is overlaid on the patient scan in red (highly influential features) to blue (uninfluential features), allowing doctors to verify that the network is focusing on actual pathology (e.g. consolidations, masses) rather than image artifacts.
